Metamask Extension Bug Sl Tp Percentages Change When Changing L

Symptom

  • Users of the Metamask extension on the Ethereum (ETH) ecosystem notice that their Stop Loss (SL) and Take Profit (TP) percentages change unexpectedly when they adjust the leverage on their trades, despite no intentional modifications to these settings.

Technical Root Cause

  • The error occurs due to a bug within the Metamask extension that incorrectly recalculates SL and TP percentages based on the leverage adjustments, instead of maintaining the user-set values.

Manual Overrides

  • To correct this issue, users can attempt the following manual overrides:
  • Manually re-enter the desired SL and TP percentages after adjusting leverage.
  • Utilize a third-party trading interface that does not rely on the Metamask extension for SL and TP management.
  • Execute a script to automatically adjust SL and TP percentages to the desired levels after leverage changes: bash # Example script to adjust SL and TP percentages leverage=$(cat leverage.txt) sl_percentage=$(cat sl.txt) tp_percentage=$(cat tp.txt) # Adjust percentages based on leverage adjusted_sl=$(echo "scale=2; $sl_percentage * (1 + $leverage)" | bc) adjusted_tp=$(echo "scale=2; $tp_percentage * (1 + $leverage)" | bc) # Apply adjustments echo $adjusted_sl > new_sl.txt echo $adjusted_tp > new_tp.txt Consider utilizing an external console for more complex scripting needs.

Prerequisites

  • Metamask extension installed and configured.
  • Basic understanding of leverage and SL/TP management in trading.
  • Familiarity with scripting languages for automation.
